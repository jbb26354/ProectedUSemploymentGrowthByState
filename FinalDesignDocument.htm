Final Design Document

  1. My map's topic, and its purpose:

    Employment outlook for the next 8 years, by state. That is, the projected increase in employment over current employment figures, by state, in the next 8 years.
  2. Map projection used:

    U.S.A. Contiguous Lambert Conformal Conic. According to the second lecture on projections, slide 30, a Lambert Conformal Conic projection is commonly used for the contiguous United States. I chose to leave the default standard parallels of 33 and 45 degrees N intact.
  3. Information Hierarchy:

    I chose my map's information hierarchy based in large part on slide 11 of the first lecture on map design fundamentals. The choropleth map, and its color scheme, was top priority. Next in importance came the map's title and legend - I had to spend some time getting the legend to look right. Lowest in importance was the ancillary text and the map's neat and frame lines.
  4. Sources of any statistical or descriptive information:

    The projected jobs website projected employment data, by state, 2012 - 2022.
  5. Data symbolization strategy:

    1. Strategy I chose:

      To save time, I deliberately chose a source dataset that would fit well in a choropleth map. The data was continuous and smooth, fitting well in a conformal, thematic setting. I wanted to keep it simple, to save time.
    2. Classification strategy:

      As you can see from the histogram, I lucked out - the data turned out not to be too skewed, with only one glaring outlier. I fixed it by snapping the breaks to the data. I went ahead and let ArcGIS apply the Jenks natural breaks classification strategy. Not classifying the data wouldn't have made sense. The summarized data as percentage ratios emphasized the pattern I wanted to show - unfortunately, I had hoped that states other than Arizona or Texas would have better showings...
    3. Single map or map series choice:

      Again, I wanted to keep it simple and similar to the Lab 7 map to save time, and chose a single map frame.
  6. Final map information hierarchy exclusions:

    This is a purely thematic map, and thus I felt that it didn't need to have certain lower-importance elements such as a scale or a north arrow. I have included my chosen standard parallels above.
